## STAGE 1 — CONNECTION
### Verify the Labor-Focused Pain

**Objective:** Confirm the prospect is trapped in manual execution — trading time for dollars with no system underneath them.

**Opening Frame (NEPQ):**
> "What does a typical week look like for you operationally — are you running the business, or is the business running you?"

**Qualifying Signals to Listen For:**
- "I'm doing everything myself"
- "I don't have a real process yet"
- "I keep losing track of receipts / clients / follow-ups"
- "I know I need to get organized but I haven't had time"

**Disqualifiers — Exit Politely If:**
- Prospect wants a done-for-you employee, not a system
- No existing revenue or active clients (not yet ready for a $5K install)
- Unwilling to implement — wants magic, not architecture

**Connection Close:**
> "Based on what you're describing, it sounds like you have the right offer but the wrong infrastructure. That's exactly what the Prime Pathwy audit is designed to identify. Can I walk you through what that looks like?"

**Output:** Qualified lead advanced to Stage 2. Log contact in `bet_history.json` client intake record or CRM equivalent.

## STAGE 2 — ENGAGEMENT
### The Prime Pathwy Audit Process

**Objective:** Demonstrate authority by diagnosing their operation before prescribing a solution. The audit IS the sales process.

**Audit Duration:** 45–60 minutes (video call or in-person)

**Audit Sections:**

| Area | What You Examine | What You're Looking For |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Revenue Flow | How they invoice, collect, track | Manual gaps, cash leakage |
| Document Stack | Receipts, contracts, onboarding | Disorganization, tax exposure |
| Client Journey | Inquiry → delivery → follow-up | No SOP, inconsistent experience |
| Time Allocation | Where hours actually go weekly | High labor-to-output ratio |
| Tools in Use | Apps, spreadsheets, notebooks | Fragmented, non-automated stack |

**Audit Delivery Standard:**
- Use Matte Black and Gold branded slides or PDF summary (Framer/Opal output)
- Present findings as a **Sovereign Score** out of 100
- Score 0–49: Critical — system does not exist
- Score 50–74: Fragmented — partial system, high drift risk
- Score 75–89: Functional — needs elevation to institutional grade
- Score 90–100: Optimized — maintenance retainer candidate

**Engagement Close:**
> "Here's what your Sovereign Score tells us — and more importantly, here's what it's costing you every week it stays this way."

**Output:** Completed audit PDF delivered. Prospect moved to Stage 3 with Sovereign Score documented.

## STAGE 3 — GAP BUILDING
### The Cost of Disorganized Paperwork

**Objective:** Make the status quo more painful than the $5,000 investment. Quantify the bleed — do not assume they feel it.

**Gap Framework — Ask These in Sequence:**

1. **Time Cost**
   > "How many hours a week do you spend on tasks that could be systematized?"
   > *(If answer is 10 hrs/wk × $50 implied value = $500/wk = $26,000/year in lost productive time)*

2. **Revenue Leakage**
   > "Have you ever lost a client or missed a follow-up because you didn't have a process in place?"
   > *(Each lost client at even $500 = cost of inaction is already compounding)*

3. **Tax Exposure**
   > "Are your receipts and deductions organized in a way your accountant could use right now?"
   > *(Disorganized records = missed deductions = real dollars left on the table)*

4. **Credibility Gap**
   > "When a high-ticket client asks how your onboarding works, what do you tell them?"
   > *(No answer = no institutional authority = price ceiling on what they can charge)*

**Gap Summary Statement:**
> "What we've identified today isn't a tools problem — it's a systems problem. And systems problems compound quietly until they either cap your income or collapse your business. The Sovereign Engine Installer exists specifically to stop that compounding."

**Output:** Prospect has verbally acknowledged the cost of inaction. Move to Stage 4.
