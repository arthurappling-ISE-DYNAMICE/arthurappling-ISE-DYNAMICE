## SECTION 3 — REVENUE WING ARCHITECTURE

AA Capital INC operates two distinct revenue wings under one legal entity.
Each wing has a defined function, a defined cash flow path, and a defined
reinvestment rule. They are not interchangeable.

---

### WING 1 — PROFESSIONAL DRIVING REVENUE
**Source:** North Bay routes · Clarence projects
**Classification:** W-2 / 1099 employment income attributed to the Principal
**Function:** Primary operating capital — funds the bi-weekly budget guardrail

**Cash Flow Path:**
```
Driving Revenue
      ↓
Personal checking account (Principal)
      ↓
Bi-weekly operating budget: $1,109.54
      ↓
Covers: Housing · Transportation · Food · Utilities · Personal obligations
```

**Governance Rule:**
The driving revenue is the foundation. It is not reinvested. It is not speculated with.
It funds the life that allows the consulting wing to operate without survival pressure.
Every dollar of driving revenue that reaches the Principal intact is a dollar of freedom
that the consulting wing does not have to manufacture under duress.

**The Firewall:**
Consulting wing expenses are never paid from driving revenue without a logged,
justified override. The wings are financially separated to protect the Principal
from a consulting revenue shortfall collapsing the operating budget.

---

### WING 2 — CONSULTING WING REVENUE
**Source:** Sovereign Engine Installer installations ($5,000 per install)
**Classification:** Business revenue — AA Capital INC
**Function:** Profit engine → surplus deployed into hard assets after budget is met

**Cash Flow Path:**
```
SEI Installation Revenue ($5,000/install)
      ↓
AA Capital INC business account
      ↓
Operating costs deducted (tools, production, delivery: ~$475/month ceiling)
      ↓
Tax reserve withheld (25% of net — see Section 5)
      ↓
Consulting Engine Profit (remaining surplus)
      ↓
Concrete and Steel Deployment (see Section 4)
```

**Governance Rule:**
The consulting wing does not fund lifestyle inflation.
Once the bi-weekly budget is met by driving revenue,
every dollar of consulting profit above operating costs
is classified as Engine Profit and deployed per the Concrete and Steel Rule.

No Engine Profit is spent on convenience, impulse, or non-compounding assets
without a written override logged in the Override Register (receipt_log.md).

> "The bi-weekly budget is the floor. It is not the ceiling.
>  Everything above the floor is building material.
>  Building material goes into assets that compound — not into expenses that don't."

---

### THE $1,109 RULE — DEFINED

The $1,109.54 bi-weekly operating budget represents the Principal's
minimum viable financial footprint — the amount required to sustain
operations, obligations, and health while the consulting wing scales.

**The Rule operates in three conditions:**

| Condition | Driving Revenue Status | Consulting Revenue | Protocol |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|----------|
| GREEN | Driving covers $1,109 guardrail | Any amount | All consulting profit → Concrete and Steel |
| YELLOW | Driving partially covers guardrail | Present | Consulting fills the gap first, surplus → Concrete and Steel |
| RED | Driving does not cover guardrail | Absent or insufficient | All revenue to operating stability. No asset deployment until GREEN restored. |

**The Rule is a priority stack, not a permission.**
It does not require the Principal to feel ready to deploy.
When the GREEN condition is met, deployment is the default action.
Inaction must be justified and logged. Deployment does not.

**Definition:**
Concrete and Steel assets are hard, compounding, non-consumable stores of value
that appreciate or generate yield independent of the Principal's active labor.

**Approved Asset Classes for Engine Profit Deployment:**

| Asset Class | Classification | Deployment Priority | Notes |

| Real Estate (REITs / fractional) | Concrete | Priority 2 | Liquid entry point while direct ownership is being structured |
