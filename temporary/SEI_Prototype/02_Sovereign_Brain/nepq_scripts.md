## CALL ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW

| Phase | Purpose | Emotional Target |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 1. Connection Questions | Neutralize sales resistance | Safety, rapport |
| 2. Problem Awareness Questions | Surface the operational pain | Recognition |
| 3. Solution Awareness Questions | Re-live the frustration of manual labor | Frustration, clarity |
| 4. Consequence Questions | Quantify the cost of inaction | Urgency, logic |
| 5. Commitment Questions | Transition to Stage 4 of SOP | Decision readiness |

**Call Duration Target:** 45–60 minutes
**Talking Ratio:** Prospect 70% · You 30%
**Core Principle:** Never answer a question with a statement. Answer with a question.

---

## PHASE 1 — CONNECTION QUESTIONS
### Break the Salesperson Stereotype

**Objective:** Open the call in a way that signals you are an advisor, not a vendor. The prospect has been pitched before. Your first 90 seconds determines whether they guard themselves or open up.

**Pre-Frame (deliver this verbatim at the top of every call):**

> "Before we get into anything — I want to be upfront with you. I'm not here to pitch you on anything today. What I'd like to do is just understand where you are in your business right now, and honestly, if what we do isn't a fit, I'll tell you that too. Does that work for you?"

*[Wait for confirmation. Do not proceed until they say yes.]*

---

**C-1 — The Neutral Opener**
> "How'd you first come across what we do — what made you want to set this time aside?"

*Purpose: Understand their intent level. High-intent prospects self-qualify here.*

---

**C-2 — The Current State Question**
> "Just so I have a clear picture — how would you describe where your business is right now, operationally?"

*Purpose: Let them narrate their reality without you framing it. Listen for language about chaos, manual work, or overwhelm.*

---

**C-3 — The Identity Calibration**
> "And when you think about the kind of business you're building — what does 'success' actually look like to you, 12 months from now?"

*Purpose: Anchor their vision. You'll return to this gap later.*

---

**C-4 — The Effort Probe**
> "How long have you been running things the way you're running them now?"

*Purpose: Longer = deeper entrenchment = larger gap to build.*

---

**C-5 — The Permission Bridge**
> "Would it be alright if I asked you a few questions about how you're managing the operational side of things? Some of them might be a little uncomfortable, but I find that's usually where the real picture is."

*Purpose: Explicit permission signals that discomfort ahead is part of the process, not an attack.*

## PHASE 2 — PROBLEM AWARENESS QUESTIONS
### Surface the Operational Pain

**Objective:** Guide the prospect to articulate their own problems — specifically around disorganized paperwork, manual processes, and lack of systems. They must say it. You must not.

---

**PA-1 — The Workflow Question**
> "Walk me through what happens when a new client comes in — from the moment they say yes, what does that process actually look like right now?"

*Listen for: improvised onboarding, verbal agreements, no contract, informal invoicing.*

---

**PA-2 — The Documentation Probe**
> "When it comes to receipts, invoices, contracts — how are you keeping track of all of that currently?"

*Common answers: "I have a folder somewhere," "I use my phone," "I really need to get that together."*
*Do not react. Just nod and say:* > "Okay. And how's that been working for you?"

---

**PA-3 — The Follow-Up Leak**
> "How often do leads or follow-ups fall through the cracks — where someone was interested and then just... nothing happened?"

*Purpose: Revenue leakage. Let them feel the loss.*

---

**PA-4 — The Time Allocation Question**
> "If you had to estimate — what percentage of your week is spent on work that actually generates revenue versus work that just keeps the lights on?"

*Purpose: Most operators are shocked when they calculate this. Let the silence sit.*

---

**PA-5 — The Tax Exposure Question**
> "Come tax season — how confident are you that you're capturing every legitimate deduction available to your business?"

*Common answer: not very confident.*
> "What does that uncertainty feel like when you're sitting down with your accountant?"

---

**PA-6 — The Repetition Probe**
> "Are there things you find yourself doing over and over again every week that you've thought — 'this should be automated by now'?"

*Purpose: Opens the door to the AI/systems conversation without you introducing it.*

---

## PHASE 3 — SOLUTION AWARENESS QUESTIONS
### Re-Live the Frustration of Labor-Focused Tasks

**Objective:** Move the prospect from intellectual awareness of their problem to emotional re-experience of it. This is where the decision becomes personal. Use their own words back at them.

---

**SA-1 — The Replay Question**
> "Think back to the last time you had to chase down a receipt or redo something because it wasn't documented the first time — what was that situation?"

*Let them tell the story. Do not interrupt.*
> "How did that make you feel — in that moment?"

---

**SA-2 — The Ceiling Question**
> "With things running the way they are right now — do you feel like you could take on significantly more clients without something breaking?"

*If no:*
> "So the business essentially has a ceiling on it — as long as the infrastructure stays the same, growth creates more problems than it solves. Is that fair to say?"

*Purpose: Get them to verbalize their own growth limitation.*

---

**SA-3 — The Identity Friction Question**
> "When you're in a conversation with a potential high-value client — someone who's used to working with organized, professional operations — how do you feel about the back-end of your business in that moment?"

*Purpose: Surface embarrassment or insecurity about not appearing institutional. This is a deep motivator.*

---

**SA-4 — The Weekend Question**
> "Be honest with me — how often does work follow you into your personal time because things aren't systematized enough to run without you?"

*Follow-up:*
> "What does that cost you — not just in hours, but personally?"

---

**SA-5 — The Prior Attempt Question**
> "Have you ever tried to put a system together before — a process, a tracker, something to organize the operation?"

*If yes:*
> "What happened with that?"

*Purpose: Establish prior failure = they know the DIY route doesn't work. Sets up the value of a professional installation.*

---

## PHASE 4 — CONSEQUENCE QUESTIONS
### Build the Gap Toward the $5,000 Investment

**Objective:** Calculate — with the prospect's own numbers — the true cost of their current operational state. The $5,000 investment must feel smaller than the problem, not larger.

---

**CQ-1 — The Time Math Question**
> "You mentioned you're spending a significant portion of your week on non-revenue work. If you had to put a rough number on it — how many hours a week?"

*[They answer — let's say 15 hours.]*

> "And what's your time worth to you — if you were billing it out, what would an hour of your focused work generate?"

*[They answer — let's say $75/hr.]*

> "So we're looking at roughly [15 × $75 = $1,125] a week in productive capacity that's currently going toward tasks that a system could handle. Does that sound right to you?"

*Let them sit with that number.*

---

**CQ-2 — The Annual Bleeding Question**
> "Over the course of a year — if nothing changes — what does that look like compounded? What are you actually leaving behind?"

*Purpose: $1,125/wk × 52 = $58,500/year in lost productivity. The $5,000 install is now less than one month of bleed.*

---

**CQ-3 — The Missed Revenue Question**
> "Think about the leads that went cold, the follow-ups that never happened, the clients you lost track of — what do you think that's cost you in revenue over the last 12 months? Even a conservative estimate."

*Do not give them a number. Make them generate it.*

---

**CQ-4 — The Tax Leak Question**
> "If you're not capturing all your deductions — and for a business operating at your level, that could easily be $3,000 to $8,000 a year in missed write-offs — what does that feel like knowing that money is just gone?"

---

**CQ-5 — The Projection Question**
> "If you're still running the business the same way 18 months from now — same manual processes, same disorganized back-end — where does that put you relative to where you said you wanted to be?"

*[Silence. Let it land.]*

> "And is that acceptable to you?"

---

**CQ-6 — The Investment Reframe**
> "So when you look at what we've walked through today — the time cost, the revenue leak, the tax exposure, the ceiling on your growth — and you set that against a one-time $5,000 system installation — how does that math feel to you now?"

*Purpose: They do the math. They arrive at the decision. You did not push.*

---

## PHASE 5 — COMMITMENT QUESTIONS
### Transition to Contract

**Objective:** Confirm decision readiness and move directly to the Stage 5 SOP (deposit and contract). Do not celebrate. Stay calm, institutional, and matter-of-fact.

---

**CM-1 — The Readiness Check**
> "Based on everything we've gone through today — how are you feeling about where you are and what needs to change?"

---

**CM-2 — The Priority Question**
> "On a scale of one to ten — how important is it to you to get this resolved in the next 30 days versus letting it ride another few months?"

*Anything below a 7:*
> "What would need to be different for this to be a 9 or 10 for you?"

---

**CM-3 — The Final Barrier Question**
> "Is there anything that would get in the way of us moving forward today — anything we haven't talked about yet?"

*Handle what surfaces. Do not skip this question.*

---

**CM-4 — The Close (Non-Pushy)**
> "Here's what I'd suggest — let's get the agreement over to you today, you review it, and if it reflects everything we've discussed, we move forward. If something doesn't line up, you tell me. Fair?"

*Do not ask "Are you ready to buy." Do not use the word "purchase."*
*The close is collaborative, not transactional.*
