[MODULE 03: INFECTION PREVENTION AND CONTROL PROTOCOLS]
- Governing Authorities: CDC / CMS Healthcare Standards
- The Chain of Infection: Implements controls to break the link between the infectious agent, reservoir, portal of exit, mode of transmission, portal of entry, and susceptible host.
- Isolation Precautions:
  1. Standard Precautions: Applied to all patient care regardless of suspected status.
  2. Contact Precautions: Required for pathogens like C. diff or MRSA. Mandates dedicated equipment and sporicidal disinfectants.
  3. Droplet Precautions: For influenza/bacterial meningitis. Mandates mask entry barriers.
  4. Airborne Precautions: For TB/Measles. Requires N95 respirators and negative-pressure AIIR rooms.
- Environmental Cleaning Hierarchy: High-touch surfaces (bed rails, call buttons, light switches) require systematic disinfection from clean-to-dirty areas to prevent cross-contamination.

[MODULE 04: OSHA HAZARD COMMUNICATION / GHS COMPLIANCE]
- Governing Standard: 29 CFR 1910.1200
- The 16-Section SDS Mandate: Safety Data Sheets must follow a strict 16-section format. Key technical verification sections include Section 4 (First-Aid Measures), Section 7 (Handling and Storage), and Section 8 (Exposure Controls/Personal Protection).
- GHS Labeling Requirements: Every hazardous chemical container must feature a label containing: Signal Words (Danger vs. Warning), Hazard Statements, Precautionary Statements, Product Identifier, and Supplier Identification.
- Pictogram Matrix: Algorithms look for verification of the 9 specific GHS hazard pictograms representing health hazards, flame, corrosion, and gas cylinders.
