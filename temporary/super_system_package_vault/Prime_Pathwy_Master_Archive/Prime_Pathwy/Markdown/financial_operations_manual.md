# Master Operating Manual  
## Section 6: Financial Operations

---

### 6.1 Invoice Systems

#### 6.1.1 System Overview  
Establish a robust, automated invoicing system to ensure accurate, timely billing and seamless integration with accounting and CRM platforms. Prioritize clarity, traceability, and compliance with contractual terms.

#### 6.1.2 Components  
- **Invoice Generation:** Automated template-driven process pulling data from project management and sales systems.  
- **Approval Workflow:** Multi-tier review (Project Lead → Finance → Client) before dispatch.  
- **Delivery Mechanisms:** Electronic delivery via encrypted email PDF and client portal access; physical copies only upon explicit request.  
- **Tracking & Reconciliation:** Real-time dashboard tracking invoice status (Pending, Sent, Paid, Overdue). Integration with bank feeds for automatic payment matching.

#### 6.1.3 Key Controls  
- Invoice numbering must be sequential and immutable.  
- Each invoice references contract ID and delivery milestones.  
- Late fee terms and early payment discounts clearly stated.  
- Archival of all invoices for a minimum of 7 years in secure, backed-up storage.

---

### 6.2 Collections Workflows

#### 6.2.1 Objective  
Maximize timely collections while preserving client relationships, using a structured escalation process.

#### 6.2.2 Workflow Steps  
1. **Pre-Due Reminder:** Automated notification 7 days prior to due date.  
2. **Due Date Day:** Invoice status updated; immediate notification to client and internal collections team.  
3. **Post-Due Follow-up:**  
   - Day 7 Past Due: First reminder via email and phone.  
   - Day 14 Past Due: Second reminder with formal letter outlining consequences of non-payment.  
   - Day 30 Past Due: Escalation to Senior Collections Specialist; initiate payment arrangement discussions.  
4. **Final Notice & Legal Referral:** After 60 days of non-payment, issue final notice and prepare account for legal collection or write-off following executive approval.

#### 6.2.3 Documentation & Reporting  
Maintain detailed logs of all communications and client responses in the collections CRM module. Weekly collections performance reports to Finance Director.

---

### 6.3 Vendor Management

#### 6.3.1 Vendor Onboarding  
- Complete due diligence including financial stability, compliance certifications, and references.  
- Formalize terms via Master Service Agreement (MSA) outlining pricing, delivery, confidentiality, and termination clauses.

#### 6.3.2 Performance Monitoring  
- Quarterly reviews assessing quality, delivery timeliness, and cost adherence.  
- Issue resolution protocols for discrepancies or performance shortfalls.

#### 6.3.3 Payment Processing  
- Centralized vendor payment system with scheduled batch payments aligned with contract terms.  
- 3-way matching (PO, Invoice, Receiving Report) mandatory before payment approval.

#### 6.3.4 Record Keeping  
All vendor contracts, communications, and payment records retained in the Vendor Management System for audit readiness.

---

### 6.4 Profitability Analysis Systems

#### 6.4.1 Framework  
Implement an integrated Profitability Analysis System (PAS) capturing revenue, direct costs, indirect costs, and overhead allocation at multiple granularity levels: project, client, and service line.

#### 6.4.2 Data Sources  
- Revenue from invoicing system.  
- Direct costs from procurement and labor tracking systems.  
- Indirect costs from general ledger allocations.

#### 6.4.3 Reporting Metrics  
- Gross Margin % per project/client/service.  
- Contribution Margin analysis.  
- Variance analysis (budget vs actual).  
- Trend analysis over rolling quarters.

#### 6.4.4 Review Cadence  
- Monthly financial reviews with project managers and finance team.  
- Quarterly executive profitability briefings focusing on strategic adjustments.

#### 6.4.5 Continuous Improvement  
Use PAS insights to:  
- Adjust pricing strategies.  
- Optimize resource allocation.  
- Identify unprofitable engagements for remediation or termination.

---

### Summary  
Section 6 establishes disciplined, transparent financial operations foundational to sustainable growth. Systems prioritize automation, auditability, and actionable intelligence, supporting Prime Pathwy’s commitment to installation excellence and operational rigor.
