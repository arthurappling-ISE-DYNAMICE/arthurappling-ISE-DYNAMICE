# Master Operating Manual  
## Section 4: Compliance

---

### Overview  
This section defines the mandatory compliance protocols for Prime Pathwy’s Sovereign System installations valued at $5,000+. Compliance ensures operational integrity, legal adherence, and risk mitigation. Procedures reflect OSHA-aligned safety standards, insurance and contractor verification workflows, and internal audit methodologies.

---

## 4.1 OSHA-Style Safety Procedures

### 4.1.1 Scope  
All on-site activities must comply with OSHA safety standards relevant to electrical, mechanical, and environmental hazards inherent in Sovereign System installations.

### 4.1.2 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)  
- Mandatory PPE includes ANSI-approved hard hats, eye protection, insulated gloves, and steel-toed boots.  
- PPE must be inspected daily and replaced if damaged.

### 4.1.3 Hazard Assessment  
- Conduct a pre-job hazard analysis (PJHA) prior to any installation or maintenance.  
- Document identified hazards and mitigation strategies on Form 4.1.3-A.

### 4.1.4 Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedures  
- Implement LOTO protocols on all electrical and mechanical equipment before service begins.  
- Verify de-energization and document using Form 4.1.4-B.

### 4.1.5 Incident Reporting  
- All incidents, near misses, and unsafe conditions must be reported within 2 hours to the Site Safety Officer.  
- Complete Incident Report Form 4.1.5-C and submit to Compliance Lead within 24 hours.

---

## 4.2 Insurance Verification Workflow

### 4.2.1 Required Insurance Types  
- General Liability: Minimum coverage $2M per occurrence.  
- Workers’ Compensation: Statutory limits as per jurisdiction.  
- Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions): Minimum $1M.

### 4.2.2 Verification Process  
1. **Pre-qualification:** Request current Certificates of Insurance (COI) from all contractors and vendors.  
2. **Validation:** Confirm policy validity dates, coverage limits, and endorsements via direct insurer contact or authorized digital portals.  
3. **Documentation:** Store verified COIs in the Compliance Management System (CMS) under folder: /Insurance/Verified.  
4. **Renewal Tracking:** Set automated reminders 30 days before expiration.  

### 4.2.3 Non-Compliance Handling  
- Suspend engagement with any party lacking valid insurance coverage until rectified.  
- Notify Contract Manager and Procurement immediately.

---

## 4.3 Contractor Verification Procedures

### 4.3.1 Pre-Engagement Checks  
- Confirm valid business licenses, certifications, and OSHA training records.  
- Validate personnel qualifications per role-specific requirements (e.g., electricians must hold Journeyman or Master Electrician licenses).

### 4.3.2 Background Screening  
- Conduct criminal background and drug screening compliant with federal and state laws.  
- Maintain records confidentially in CMS under /Contractors/BackgroundChecks.

### 4.3.3 Orientation and Training  
- Provide mandatory Prime Pathwy safety and operational orientation before site access is granted.  
- Document attendance with signed acknowledgment forms.

### 4.3.4 Performance Monitoring  
- Implement periodic compliance and performance reviews at 30, 60, and 90 days.  
- Use Contractor Performance Evaluation Form 4.3.4-D.

---

## 4.4 Audit Procedures

### 4.4.1 Audit Objectives  
- Ensure continuous adherence to OSHA standards, insurance requirements, and contractor compliance.  
- Identify potential gaps and implement corrective actions promptly.

### 4.4.2 Audit Frequency  
- Conduct full compliance audits quarterly.  
- Perform targeted audits post-incident or upon regulatory updates.

### 4.4.3 Audit Process  
1. **Planning:** Define scope, objectives, and audit team.  
2. **Document Review:** Verify all required documentation is current and complete (PPE logs, COIs, training records).  
3. **Site Inspection:** Physically inspect installation sites for safety compliance and procedural adherence.  
4. **Interviews:** Engage with on-site personnel and contractors regarding compliance awareness.  
5. **Reporting:** Compile findings in Audit Report Form 4.4.3-E, categorizing issues by severity.

### 4.4.4 Corrective Action  
- Assign responsibility and deadlines for remediation of audit findings.  
- Verify closure of corrective actions within 30 days and document in CMS.
