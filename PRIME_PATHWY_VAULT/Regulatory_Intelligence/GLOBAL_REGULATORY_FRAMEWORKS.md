## SECTION 1 — U.S. FEDERAL REGULATORY FRAMEWORKS

### 1.1 Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)

**Overview:** The FAR is the primary regulation governing all U.S. federal government procurement. It is codified in Title 48 of the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR).

**Key FAR Parts for Prime Pathwy:**

| FAR Part | Title | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Part 1 | Federal Acquisition Regulations System | Foundation |
| Part 4 | Administrative and Information Matters | Compliance records |
| Part 9 | Contractor Qualifications | Eligibility requirements |
| Part 12 | Acquisition of Commercial Items | Simplified procedures |
| Part 13 | Simplified Acquisition Procedures | <$250K contracts |
| Part 15 | Contracting by Negotiation | Competitive proposals |
| Part 19 | Small Business Programs | Set-aside eligibility |
| Part 22 | Application of Labor Laws | Employment compliance |
| Part 31 | Contract Cost Principles | Cost accounting |
| Part 52 | Solicitation Provisions and Contract Clauses | Standard clauses |

**Critical Compliance Requirements:**
- SAM.gov registration (mandatory, renew annually)
- Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) — replaced DUNS in 2022
- Representations and Certifications (Reps & Certs) — annual update
- System for Award Management (SAM) exclusions check
- Buy American Act compliance (where applicable)
- Trade Agreements Act compliance (where applicable)

### 1.3 Small Business Administration (SBA) Regulations

**8(a) Business Development Program:**
- Eligibility: Socially and economically disadvantaged individuals (51%+ ownership)
- Program duration: 9 years (4-year developmental + 5-year transitional)
- Benefits: Sole-source contracts up to $4.5M (services) / $7M (manufacturing)
- Annual revenue cap: $4M (developmental) / $6.5M (transitional) for most industries
- Application: certify.sba.gov

**HUBZone Program:**
- Eligibility: Located in Historically Underutilized Business Zone, 35%+ employees in HUBZone
- Benefits: 10% price evaluation preference, sole-source up to $4.5M
- Annual contract goal: 3% of federal spending

**WOSB/EDWOSB Program:**
- Eligibility: 51%+ women-owned and controlled
- Benefits: Set-aside contracts in underrepresented industries
- Annual contract goal: 5% of federal spending

**SDVOSB Program:**
- Eligibility: 51%+ service-disabled veteran owned and controlled
- Benefits: Set-aside contracts, sole-source up to $4.5M
- Annual contract goal: 3% of federal spending

## SECTION 2 — INTERNATIONAL REGULATORY FRAMEWORKS

### 2.1 United Kingdom — Procurement Act 2023

**Overview:** Replaced EU-derived procurement regulations post-Brexit. Effective February 2024.

**Key Changes:**
- Single consolidated framework (replaced PCR 2015, UCR 2016, CCR 2016)
- New procurement procedures: Open, Competitive Flexible, Limited Tendering
- Enhanced transparency requirements (new digital platform)
- Stronger SME participation requirements
- New debarment regime
- Enhanced social value requirements

**Compliance Requirements for UK Government Contracts:**
- Companies House registration
- Tax compliance certificates
- Insurance certificates (public liability, professional indemnity)
- Financial standing evidence
- Technical capability evidence

### 2.2 European Union — Public Procurement Directives

**Primary Directives:**
- Directive 2014/24/EU — Public sector procurement
- Directive 2014/25/EU — Utilities procurement
- Directive 2014/23/EU — Concession contracts

**Key Thresholds (2024–2025):**
- Central government services/supplies: €143,000
- Sub-central government: €221,000
- Utilities: €443,000
- Works contracts: €5,538,000

**GDPR Compliance (Critical for AI Services):**
- All AI systems processing EU citizen data must comply with GDPR
- Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) required
- Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) required
- Data residency requirements (EU data must stay in EU)
- Right to explanation for automated decisions (Article 22)

**EU AI Act (Effective 2024–2026):**
- High-risk AI systems require conformity assessment
- Prohibited AI practices (social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance)
- Transparency requirements for AI systems
- **Opportunity:** EU AI Act compliance consulting is a growing market

### 2.3 Australia — Commonwealth Procurement Rules (CPRs)

**Overview:** Govern all Commonwealth entity procurement. Updated regularly.

**Key Requirements:**
- Value for money (primary principle)
- Open and effective competition
- Ethical behavior
- Accountability and transparency
- Efficient, effective, economical, and ethical use of resources

**Thresholds:**
- Open tender required: AUD $80,000 (goods/services) / AUD $7.5M (construction)
- Indigenous procurement policy: 3% of contracts reserved

---

### 2.4 UAE — Federal Procurement Law

**Overview:** Federal Law No. 6 of 2008 governs federal procurement. Emirate-specific regulations also apply.

**Key Requirements:**
- UAE company registration (required for most contracts)
- Emiratisation compliance (percentage of UAE national employees)
- Local content requirements (increasing)
- Anti-corruption compliance
- Financial standing requirements

**Dubai Specific:**
- eSupply portal registration required
- Dubai Chamber of Commerce membership often required
- ISO certifications valued

---

### 2.5 Singapore — Government Procurement Act

**Overview:** Singapore's procurement is governed by the Government Procurement Act and GeBIZ platform.

**Key Features:**
- GeBIZ registration required for all government suppliers
- Transparent, competitive process
- WTO GPA signatory — open to international competition
- Government Technology Agency (GovTech) manages technology procurement
- Smart Nation initiative drives AI/technology procurement
