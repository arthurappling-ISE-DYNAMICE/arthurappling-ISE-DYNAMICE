## 1. SBA LOAN PROGRAMS

The Small Business Administration (SBA) guarantees loans made by approved lenders. The guarantee reduces lender risk, enabling access to capital that would otherwise be unavailable.

### SBA 7(a) Loan Program
The primary SBA program for working capital, equipment, and business acquisition.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maximum Loan Amount | $5,000,000 |
| SBA Guarantee | Up to 85% (loans ≤$150K); up to 75% (loans >$150K) |
| Use of Proceeds | Working capital, equipment, real estate, debt refinancing |
| Maturity | Up to 10 years (working capital); up to 25 years (real estate) |
| Interest Rate | Prime + 2.75% (variable) or fixed; currently ~10–11% range |
| Collateral | Required if available; SBA cannot decline solely for lack |
| Down Payment | Typically 10–20% for business acquisition |
| Key Eligibility | For-profit; operates in US; owner has invested equity; exhausted other financing |

### SBA 504 Loan Program
For fixed assets (real estate, heavy equipment). Structured as a 3-party transaction.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maximum Loan Amount | $5,000,000 (up to $5.5M for manufacturing/energy) |
| Structure | 50% Bank + 40% SBA/CDC + 10% Borrower |
| Use of Proceeds | Real estate, construction, heavy equipment (10+ year life) |
| Maturity | 10, 20, or 25 years |
| Interest Rate | Below-market fixed rate (SBA portion) |
| Collateral | The financed asset serves as primary collateral |
| Key Eligibility | Net worth <$15M; average net income <$5M (last 2 years) |

### SBA Microloan Program
For very small businesses and startups needing smaller amounts.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maximum Loan Amount | $50,000 |
| Average Loan Size | ~$13,000 |
| Use of Proceeds | Working capital, inventory, supplies, equipment |
| Maturity | Up to 6 years |
| Interest Rate | 8–13% |
| Administered By | Nonprofit intermediary lenders |

## 2. DSCR (DEBT SERVICE COVERAGE RATIO) INTELLIGENCE

DSCR is the primary underwriting metric used by commercial lenders to evaluate a business's ability to service debt.

**Formula:** `DSCR = Net Operating Income (NOI) / Annual Debt Service`

### DSCR Thresholds by Lender Type

| Lender Type | Minimum DSCR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| SBA 7(a) | 1.25x | Standard SBA requirement |
| SBA 504 | 1.25x | Standard SBA requirement |
| Conventional Bank | 1.20x – 1.25x | Conservative underwriting |
| CDFI / Mission Lender | 1.10x – 1.15x | More flexible for underserved borrowers |
| Hard Money / Bridge | 1.00x – 1.10x | Short-term; higher rate |
| DSCR Rental Loans | 1.00x – 1.25x | Based on property cash flow only |

### DSCR Calculation Example

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross Annual Revenue | $500,000 |
| Operating Expenses (excl. debt) | ($350,000) |
| **Net Operating Income (NOI)** | **$150,000** |
| Annual Debt Service (proposed loan) | ($100,000) |
| **DSCR** | **1.50x** |

*A DSCR of 1.50x means the business generates $1.50 for every $1.00 of debt payment. This is a strong application.*

## 3. EQUIPMENT FINANCING

Equipment financing is the fastest path to capital for service businesses. The equipment itself serves as collateral, reducing lender risk.

### Equipment Finance Structures

| Structure | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment Loan | Borrow to purchase; own at end of term | Long-life equipment (trucks, floor machines) |
| Equipment Lease (Capital) | Lease with $1 buyout at end | Tax benefit; preserve cash |
| Equipment Lease (Operating) | Lease with fair market value buyout | Shorter-term use; off-balance-sheet |
| Equipment Line of Credit | Revolving credit for equipment purchases | Frequent equipment needs |
| SBA 504 Equipment | Long-term fixed-rate for heavy equipment | Equipment >$100K with 10+ year life |

## 5. UNDERWRITING READINESS CHECKLIST

Before approaching any lender, ensure these documents are prepared:

| Document | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3 Years Business Tax Returns | Income verification | Must show positive NOI |
| 3 Years Personal Tax Returns | Personal financial picture | Required for SBA |
| Current Year P&L (YTD) | Current performance | Must be within 90 days |
| Current Balance Sheet | Assets/liabilities snapshot | Must be within 90 days |
| Business Bank Statements (12 months) | Cash flow verification | All accounts |
| Personal Bank Statements (3 months) | Personal cash reserves | Required for SBA |
| Business Debt Schedule | All current obligations | Includes all loans, leases |
| Business Plan / Use of Proceeds | Loan purpose | Required for SBA |
| AR Aging Report | Receivables quality | Shows collection history |
| Government Contracts (if applicable) | Revenue predictability | Strongest collateral for service businesses |
