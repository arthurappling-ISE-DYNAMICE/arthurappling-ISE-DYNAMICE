Leadership in operations is less about authority and more about clarity. Define explicit decision rights—who decides what, when, and how. This removes ambiguity and accelerates execution. 

Execution demands discipline in feedback loops: measure outcomes, compare against standards, and adjust promptly. Without this cycle, plans stagnate.

Logistics optimization hinges on reducing handoffs. Each transfer of responsibility is a risk vector. Streamline processes so that end-to-end ownership is concentrated, not diffused.

Action Step: Map your critical workflows. Identify decision points and assign clear owners. Then, establish real-time feedback mechanisms to detect deviations early. This is how control scales without adding layers.
