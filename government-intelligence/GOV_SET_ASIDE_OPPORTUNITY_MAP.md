## SECTION 1 — SET-ASIDE PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Set-aside contracts are federal contracts reserved exclusively for small businesses meeting specific criteria. They represent the single most powerful competitive advantage available to a small business in the federal marketplace. When a contract is set aside, the number of eligible bidders drops dramatically — often from hundreds to fewer than ten.

The federal government is legally required to award 23% of all federal contract dollars to small businesses annually. Agencies that fail to meet this goal face internal pressure from the SBA. This creates a structural incentive for contracting officers to actively seek qualified small businesses.

## SECTION 3 — 8(a) PROGRAM DEEP INTELLIGENCE

### Program Structure
The 8(a) program is a 9-year business development program. The first 4 years are the "developmental stage" and the last 5 years are the "transitional stage." Participants receive dedicated Business Opportunity Specialists (BOS) who actively help them find and win contracts.

### Sole-Source Authority
The most powerful feature of 8(a) is the sole-source authority. A contracting officer can award a contract directly to an 8(a) firm — without competition — up to $4.5 million for services and $7 million for manufacturing. This means a single relationship with a contracting officer can produce a multi-million dollar contract with zero competition.

### Eligibility Criteria (Verified 2026)
- Business must be at least 51% owned by a socially and economically disadvantaged U.S. citizen
- Owner's personal net worth must be $850,000 or less (excluding equity in primary residence and business)
- Owner's adjusted gross income must be $400,000 or less (3-year average)
- Owner's total assets must be $6.5 million or less
- Business must have been in operation for at least 2 years
- Business must demonstrate potential for success
- Owner must demonstrate good character

### Application Process
1. Create account at certify.sba.gov
2. Complete online application (approximately 20–40 hours of documentation)
3. Submit financial statements, tax returns (3 years), business documents
4. SBA review period: typically 90 days
5. If approved, assigned to local SBA district office

### 2026 Program Alert
In March 2026, the SBA moved to terminate over 620 firms from the 8(a) program for failure to provide financial data. This indicates increased scrutiny and compliance enforcement. Applicants must maintain clean financial records and respond promptly to SBA requests.

## SECTION 4 — HUBZONE PROGRAM INTELLIGENCE

HUBZone (Historically Underutilized Business Zone) certification provides a 10% price preference in competitive bidding. This means a HUBZone firm can win even if their bid is up to 10% higher than a non-HUBZone competitor.

### Eligibility
- Business must be located in a HUBZone (check map at sba.gov/hubzone)
- At least 35% of employees must reside in a HUBZone
- Business must be small per SBA size standards

### Northern California HUBZone Areas
Many areas in Solano County, Napa County, and the broader North Bay qualify as HUBZones due to lower median household income levels. Verify specific addresses at the SBA HUBZone map tool.

## SECTION 5 — CALIFORNIA DIR REGISTRATION (PREVAILING WAGE)

Any business performing public works construction, alteration, demolition, installation, or repair work in California on a contract over $1,000 must be registered with the California Department of Industrial Relations (DIR).

**Applies to:** Painting contractors, hauling contractors (when hauling construction debris), facility maintenance contractors performing repair work.

**Registration:** dir.ca.gov/Public-Works/PublicWorks.html
**Fee:** $400 per year
**Consequence of Non-Registration:** Disqualification from public works bids; potential civil penalties.

## SECTION 6 — EMERGENCY PROCUREMENT INTELLIGENCE

Emergency procurement is the highest-margin, fastest-award government contracting opportunity available. During declared disasters (fire, flood, earthquake), normal competitive bidding requirements are suspended. Agencies can award contracts directly to pre-registered vendors.

### Pre-Registration Targets
| Agency | Program | URL | Strategic Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cal OES | Disaster Vendor Registry | caloes.ca.gov | State emergency contracts — debris hauling, transport |
| FEMA Region 9 | FEMA Vendor Registry | fema.gov | Federal emergency contracts — highest margins |
| USACE (Army Corps) | APTIS Pre-Qualification | usace.army.mil | Debris removal, emergency construction |

**Northern California Context:** Wildfire season creates annual emergency procurement cycles in Napa, Sonoma, and surrounding counties. A pre-registered hauling or debris removal company is positioned to receive direct awards during every fire season without competition.
