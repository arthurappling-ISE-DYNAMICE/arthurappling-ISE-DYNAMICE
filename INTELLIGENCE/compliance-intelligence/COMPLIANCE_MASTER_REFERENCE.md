## 1. CAL/OSHA COMPLIANCE (CALIFORNIA SPECIFIC)

California OSHA (Cal/OSHA) requirements are stricter than federal OSHA. Strict adherence is required for all commercial cleaning, facilities maintenance, and debris removal operations.

### Required Written Programs
1. **IIPP (Injury and Illness Prevention Program)** [1]
   - Mandatory for all California employers.
   - Must be written, implemented, and maintained.
   - Must include hazard identification, correction, and employee training.
2. **Hazard Communication Program (HazCom)**
   - Required due to chemical use (cleaning supplies, solvents).
   - Must include SDS (Safety Data Sheets) access for all employees.
   - Requires container labeling and chemical safety training.
3. **Bloodborne Pathogens Standard**
   - Required for janitorial staff cleaning restrooms or medical facilities.
   - Requires written Exposure Control Plan.
   - Hepatitis B vaccination must be offered to exposed employees.
4. **Heat Illness Prevention Plan**
   - Required for outdoor workers (landscaping, pressure washing, debris removal).
   - Triggers at 80°F (water, shade, rest).

### Required Training
- Initial hire safety orientation.
- New hazard training (e.g., new chemical introduction).
- Annual refresher training for Bloodborne Pathogens and HazCom.
- *Documentation:* All training must be documented with signatures and kept for 3 years.

## 2. CALIFORNIA LABOR LAW & PREVAILING WAGE

### Prevailing Wage (Public Works) [2]
- **Trigger:** Any public works contract over $1,000.
- **Requirement:** Must pay DIR-determined prevailing wage rates for the specific craft (e.g., Janitor, Laborer).
- **Registration:** Contractor must register with DIR ($400 annual fee) before bidding.
- **Reporting:** Certified payroll records must be submitted electronically to the DIR eCPR system weekly or monthly.
- **Apprenticeship:** Contracts over $30,000 require hiring DIR-approved apprentices.

### Wage & Hour Laws
- **Minimum Wage:** Must comply with California state minimum wage or local city/county ordinance (whichever is higher).
- **Overtime:** 1.5x pay over 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week; 2.0x pay over 12 hours/day.
- **Meal/Rest Breaks:** 30-minute unpaid meal break before the end of the 5th hour; 10-minute paid rest break per 4 hours worked.

## 3. EPA & ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE

### Hazardous Waste Disposal
- **Trigger:** Transporting or disposing of hazardous materials (chemicals, biohazards, specific debris).
- **Requirement:** Must use licensed hazardous waste haulers.
- **Manifests:** Must retain hazardous waste manifests indefinitely.

### Stormwater Compliance (Pressure Washing)
- **Trigger:** Pressure washing building exteriors, parking lots, or sidewalks.
- **Requirement:** Wash water cannot enter storm drains. Must be reclaimed, filtered, and discharged into the sanitary sewer (with permission) or hauled away.
- **Violation:** Significant fines from local water boards or EPA.

## 5. CONTRACTOR LICENSING (CALIFORNIA) [4]

### CSLB (Contractors State License Board)
- **Janitorial Services:** Generally do *not* require a CSLB license if the work is strictly cleaning.
- **Facilities Maintenance/Handyman:** Requires a CSLB license if the total cost of a project (labor + materials) is $500 or more.
- **Relevant Licenses:**
  - Class B: General Building Contractor
  - Class C-61 / D-63: Construction Clean-up
  - Class C-27: Landscaping Contractor

### Business Licensing
- Must hold a valid business license in the city/county where the business is headquartered.
- Must hold a business license in *every* city where work is performed.
