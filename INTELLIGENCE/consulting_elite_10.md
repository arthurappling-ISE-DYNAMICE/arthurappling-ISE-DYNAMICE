## Purpose

This script is the opening weapon of every Prime Pathwy Sovereign System consultation.

It is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic. The prospect does the talking. You do the listening. By the end of this conversation, the prospect has talked themselves into understanding two things:

1. Their current operation is costing them more than $5,000/year in chaos.
2. Prime Pathwy is the only logical next step.

> **The rule:** Never tell them what they have. Ask until they tell you.

---

## Script Architecture

```
CONNECTION → PROBLEM AWARENESS → SOLUTION AWARENESS → THE $5,000 GAP → CLOSE
```

Each section builds pressure on the prior one. Do not skip sections. Do not rush.  
Silence after a question is not awkward — it is them doing the work for you.

---

## SECTION 1 — CONNECTION QUESTIONS
*Goal: Break the ice. Establish peer-level rapport. Position as a fellow operator, not a salesperson.*

> Tone: Calm. Curious. Unhurried. You are not trying to impress them.

---

**Q1.** "Hey [Name] — before we get into anything, tell me a little about what you're running. What does a normal week look like for you?"

*(Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Nod. Take notes.)*

---

**Q2.** "How long have you been in [Hauling / Painting / Logistics]? Did you build this from scratch or take something over?"

*(This establishes pride. They built something. Acknowledge it before you diagnose it.)*

---

**Q3.** "What's the part of the business you actually enjoy? Like, if you strip away all the headaches — what made you get into this in the first place?"

*(This creates contrast. They'll remember why they started. Then they'll feel the gap between that vision and their current reality.)*

---

**Q4.** "And right now — are you pretty much running this thing yourself, or do you have people helping you manage the day-to-day?"

*(Maps the dependency structure. If the answer is "mostly me" — the problem is already on the table.)*

---

## SECTION 2 — PROBLEM AWARENESS QUESTIONS
*Goal: Surface the pain of manual, paper-based, owner-dependent operations. Make them re-live it. Do not solve anything yet.*

> Tone: Empathetic. Slightly concerned. You've seen this before. You're not surprised.

---

**Q5.** "Walk me through how a job actually gets booked right now. Like from the moment someone calls you — what happens next?"

*(This is the autopsy question. Listen for: texts, spreadsheets, phone calls, verbal agreements, paper invoices. Every one of those is a system failure.)*

---

**Q6.** "When you've got multiple jobs running at the same time — how are you keeping track of everything? Is there a system, or is it mostly in your head?"

*(The phrase "mostly in my head" is the answer you're waiting for. When they say it, pause. Let it land. Then:)*

> "So if something happened to you — if you got sick for two weeks — what would happen to the business?"

*(This single follow-up question has more selling power than any pitch deck.)*

---

**Q7.** "What about invoicing — how long does it usually take you to get paid after a job is done? And do you ever have to chase people down for it?"

*(Maps the revenue leakage. Most operators are losing 10–30% of billable revenue to delayed or forgotten invoicing.)*

---

**Q8.** "Be honest with me — how many hours a week do you think you're spending on stuff that has nothing to do with actually doing the work? Scheduling, paperwork, follow-ups, answering the same questions over and over?"

*(This is the chaos quantification question. Get a number. 10 hours? 20 hours? Write it down. You'll use it in Section 4.)*

---

## SECTION 3 — SOLUTION AWARENESS QUESTIONS
*Goal: Introduce the concept of AI automation and Sovereign Systems without pitching. Let them arrive at the conclusion themselves.*

> Tone: Thoughtful. Forward-looking. You're not selling — you're expanding their awareness.

---

**Q9.** "Have you ever looked into what other operators in your space are doing to get out of the day-to-day? Like, do you know anyone running a similar business who isn't stuck in the middle of everything?"

*(If yes: "What are they doing differently?" — they'll describe a system.)*  
*(If no: "That's actually pretty common at this stage. There's a reason for that — and it's fixable.")*

---

**Q10.** "What if I told you that everything you just described — the scheduling, the follow-ups, the invoicing, the crew communication — all of it could run on its own? Not perfectly, but 80% of it without you touching it. Would that change anything for you?"

*(Wait for the answer. If they say "yeah that would be huge" — you're in. If they push back, ask:)*

> "What would have to be true for you to believe that was actually possible?"

*(This removes the objection. They'll tell you exactly what they need to see.)*

---

**Q11.** "The operators who've already made this shift — they're not working less because they're lazy. They're working less on the business so they can work more on the business. Does that distinction make sense to you?"

*(This reframes automation from "cheating" to "strategy." Small business owners feel guilt about stepping back. This removes it.)*

---

**Q12.** "On a scale of 1 to 10 — how much longer are you willing to run it the way you're running it right now?"

*(This is the closing question of Section 3. Anything below a 7 means they've accepted the pain. Anything 7 or above means they're resigned to it — which is actually more urgent. Either way, move to Section 4.)*

---

## SECTION 4 — THE $5,000 GAP
*Goal: Anchor the investment to the cost of their current chaos. Make the ROI undeniable before the price is ever spoken.*

> Tone: Direct. Clinical. You are now the architect, not the consultant.

---

**The Setup:**

"Okay — I want to show you something real quick. You told me you're spending about [X hours/week] on admin, scheduling, follow-up, and paperwork. Let me run a quick number."

---

**The Math (deliver this verbally, step by step):**

> "[X hours/week] × 52 weeks = [annual hours lost]  
> At your effective rate of even $[Y/hour], that's [$Z/year] in time cost alone.  
> That doesn't include the revenue you're not capturing because follow-ups are falling through the cracks.  
> Most operators in your position are leaving $40,000 to $100,000 on the table every year — not because they're not working hard, but because the system isn't working for them."

*(Pause. Let the number sit.)*

---

**The Gap Question:**

**Q13.** "So if we built you a system that recaptured even 30% of that — automated your dispatch, your invoicing, your follow-up, your client communication — and handed it to you fully documented and 100% yours to own... what would that be worth to you over the next 12 months?"

*(Let them answer. They will say a number. That number will almost always be higher than $5,000.)*

---

**The Anchor:**

"The Sovereign System installation is a one-time investment. You walk away with a fully operational, documented system — managed GitHub repo, operational manual, custom domain, every automation configured and running. It's not a subscription. It's not a retainer. It's yours."

---

**Q14.** "Does the investment make sense when you see it against what you're currently losing?"

*(This is not a close. It is a permission question. You are asking them to confirm their own logic. If they say yes — proceed. If they hesitate:)*

> "What part of the math doesn't add up for you?"

*(Address that one thing. Do not defend the price. Return to their chaos number.)*

---

**Q15.** "What would need to happen in the next 30 days for you to feel like this was the right decision?"

*(This is the close. It surfaces the final objection, maps the decision timeline, and positions you as a partner — not a vendor. Whatever they say is your next action item.)*

2. Attach a **Sovereign Blueprint Preview** — a visual sketch of what their system will look like
3. Schedule the **Step 1 Operational Autopsy** within 7 days of verbal agreement
4. Log all notes from this session into the client's folder in the Command Center

## NEPQ Principles — Prime Pathwy Standard

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| Never pitch — diagnose | Every question surfaces a problem they name themselves |
| Silence is leverage | After a question, wait. The discomfort belongs to them. |
