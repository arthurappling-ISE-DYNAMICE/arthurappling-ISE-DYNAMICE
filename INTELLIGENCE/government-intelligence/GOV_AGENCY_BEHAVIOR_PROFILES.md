## PROCUREMENT OFFICER RELATIONSHIP MAPPING PROTOCOL

Building direct relationships with procurement officers is the highest-leverage activity in government contracting. The following protocol should be executed annually:

1. **Identify the Procurement Officer** for each target agency using the agency's procurement portal or by calling the purchasing department directly.
2. **Send a Capability Statement** via email with a brief introduction. Do not pitch — simply introduce the company and its NAICS codes.
3. **Request a Capabilities Briefing** — a 15-minute call or meeting to discuss upcoming procurement needs. Many agencies will accept this.
4. **Attend Pre-Bid Conferences** — agencies hold these before major RFPs. Attendance signals seriousness and allows direct Q&A with the contracting officer.
5. **Track Contract Expiration Dates** — use USAspending.gov to find current contracts and their expiration dates. Contact the agency 6–12 months before expiration.
6. **Document every interaction** — date, name, title, topic, outcome. This becomes institutional memory for future bids.
