## SECTION 1 — STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

Government procurement represents the single most durable revenue stream available to a sovereign one-man enterprise. Unlike commercial contracts, which depend on client budgets and economic cycles, government contracts are funded by appropriations that renew annually and follow predictable cycles. A single janitorial or hauling contract at the county level can generate $50,000–$500,000 per year in recurring revenue. A federal contract under NAICS 561720 can span five years with automatic option-year renewals.

The core strategic insight is this: **government agencies do not search for vendors — they search for registered, compliant, visible entities in their procurement systems.** Invisibility is the primary reason small businesses fail to win government contracts. The solution is not better marketing — it is systematic registration, correct NAICS code assignment, and agency-specific positioning.

## SECTION 5 — CONTRACT PATTERN ANALYSIS

### Janitorial Services (NAICS 561720)
Federal agencies award hundreds of millions annually under this code. The standard contract structure is a **base year + four option years** (5-year total). Performance determines option-year exercise. Key behavioral pattern: agencies rarely switch vendors mid-contract unless performance fails. **First win is the hardest; subsequent renewals are near-automatic.**

### Debris Hauling / Solid Waste (NAICS 562111)
Municipal contracts dominate this space. Counties and cities typically rebid every 1–3 years. Emergency debris hauling (storm, fire, flood response) is a separate procurement track — FEMA-coordinated, faster awards, higher margins.

### Painting / Coating (NAICS 238320)
GSA and Army Corps of Engineers are the primary federal buyers. State and county facilities maintenance departments are the primary local buyers. Prevailing wage requirements apply to most public works painting contracts over $1,000 in California (DIR registration required).

### Transportation / Logistics (NAICS 484110, 484121)
DoD and USPS are the largest federal buyers. Municipal transit authorities and school districts are the largest local buyers. School district transportation contracts are particularly stable — multi-year terms, predictable volume, low competition from large carriers.

## SECTION 6 — COMMON REJECTION REASONS (AGENCY BEHAVIOR INTELLIGENCE)

| Rejection Reason | Frequency | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| SAM.gov registration expired | Very High | Set calendar reminder 60 days before annual renewal |
| Missing or incorrect NAICS codes | High | Audit SAM profile quarterly |
| Capability Statement not submitted | High | Always attach to bid submission |
| Insurance certificate expired | High | Maintain 30-day advance renewal protocol |
| Incomplete past performance section | Medium | Document every commercial job as past performance |
| Proposal not responsive to all requirements | Medium | Use a compliance matrix for every RFP |
| Prevailing wage non-compliance (California) | Medium | Register with CA DIR before bidding public works |
| Bond capacity insufficient | Low | Establish surety relationship before bidding |
