## PURPOSE

This document captures recurring language patterns found in government RFPs for janitorial, facilities maintenance, debris cleanup, and public works contracts. Use these patterns to:

1. Identify relevant opportunities faster during bid searches.
2. Mirror agency language in proposal responses (compliance scoring).
3. Anticipate evaluation criteria before reading the full RFP.
4. Flag incumbent-protection language (sole-source, brand-specific specs).

---

## SECTION 1 — SCOPE OF WORK LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### Janitorial / Custodial

| Pattern Phrase | Meaning / Signal |
|---|---|
| "daily, weekly, and monthly cleaning schedules" | Recurring contract; predictable scope |
| "restroom sanitation and consumable restocking" | Includes supplies — price accordingly |
| "floor care including stripping, waxing, and buffing" | Specialized equipment required |
| "carpet extraction and spot cleaning" | Wet extraction equipment needed |
| "window cleaning — interior and exterior" | Height access equipment may be needed |
| "post-event cleanup" | Variable scope; may need surge staffing |
| "green cleaning products required" | EPA Safer Choice or Green Seal certified products |
| "LEED-certified building maintenance" | Strict chemical and waste protocols |
| "biohazard cleanup as needed" | Requires OSHA bloodborne pathogen training |
| "24/7 emergency response availability" | On-call requirement; price premium |

### Debris / Cleanup / Public Works

| Pattern Phrase | Meaning / Signal |
|---|---|
| "illegal dumping abatement" | Recurring municipal contract; high volume |
| "right-of-way debris removal" | Caltrans or city public works |
| "storm drain clearing and maintenance" | Environmental compliance required |
| "graffiti abatement services" | Specialized chemicals; city contracts |
| "encampment cleanup and remediation" | Sensitive work; biohazard protocols |
| "post-disaster debris removal" | FEMA-eligible; emergency procurement |
| "construction site cleanup" | Contractor coordination required |
| "hazardous material identification and removal" | Requires licensed hazmat contractor |
| "pressure washing of public infrastructure" | High-volume recurring opportunity |

## SECTION 2 — EVALUATION CRITERIA PATTERNS

| Criterion | Typical Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Technical Approach / Methodology | 30–40% | Detailed work plan required |
| Qualifications / Experience | 20–30% | References + past performance critical |
| Price / Cost | 20–40% | Competitive pricing; unit rates preferred |
| Small Business / DVBE Status | 5–10% | Certification adds points |
| Local Hire / Workforce | 5–10% | Some agencies require local labor |
| Staffing Plan | 10–20% | Supervisor ratios, training records |
| Equipment List | 5–10% | Owned vs. leased; age of fleet |
| Insurance Compliance | Pass/Fail | Must meet minimums or disqualified |
| References | Pass/Fail or scored | 3–5 verifiable references required |

## SECTION 3 — COMPLIANCE LANGUAGE PATTERNS

| Pattern Phrase | Compliance Requirement |
|---|---|
| "prevailing wage rates per DIR determination" | California DIR wage schedule; certified payroll |
| "living wage ordinance applies" | City-specific wage floor above minimum wage |
| "certified payroll records required" | Weekly submission to agency |
| "DIR Public Works registration required" | Must register at https://www.dir.ca.gov/pwc |
| "apprenticeship utilization required" | Must use DIR-approved apprentices |
| "SB/DVBE participation goal: 25%/3%" | Subcontracting goals; good faith effort required |
| "proof of workers' compensation required" | Certificate of insurance at contract execution |
| "background check required for all personnel" | DOJ/FBI fingerprinting; LiveScan |
| "drug-free workplace certification" | STD 21 form required |
| "non-discrimination certification" | STD 17 or equivalent |
| "conflict of interest disclosure" | FPPC Form 700 or equivalent |

## SECTION 4 — INCUMBENT PROTECTION LANGUAGE (RED FLAGS)

| Pattern | Signal |
|---|---|
| "must have minimum 5 years experience with [specific agency]" | Written to favor incumbent |
| "contractor must be familiar with existing systems" | Incumbent advantage |
| "transition period not to exceed 2 weeks" | Short transition favors incumbent |
| "proprietary equipment or software required" | Sole-source justification |
| "no substitutions accepted" | Brand-specific spec; challenge if possible |
| "award to lowest responsible bidder" | Price competition; incumbent may cut price |
| "best value selection" | Opens door for quality-based competition |

## SECTION 6 — PROPOSAL RESPONSE FRAMEWORK

**Standard Government Proposal Structure:**

1. Cover Letter (1 page — executive summary)
2. Table of Contents
3. Section A: Understanding of Scope
4. Section B: Technical Approach / Work Plan
5. Section C: Qualifications and Experience
6. Section D: Key Personnel and Staffing Plan
7. Section E: Equipment and Resources
8. Section F: Quality Control Plan
9. Section G: Safety Plan
10. Section H: References (3–5 verifiable)
11. Section I: Certifications (SB, DVBE, insurance, licenses)
12. Section J: Price Schedule / Cost Proposal (separate sealed envelope if required)
