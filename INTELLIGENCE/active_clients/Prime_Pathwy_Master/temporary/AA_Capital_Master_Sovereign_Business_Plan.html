The AB 2019 Opportunity · Solano County · State Procurement Pipeline

The AB 2019 Legislative Framework

California's legislative environment has created a structural mandate for Small Business participation in state government procurement. Under California Government Code §14837 et seq. (the foundational SB mandate), state agencies are required to award 25% of contract dollars to certified Small Businesses. Assembly Bill 2019 reinforced and expanded the procurement preference framework, increasing the accountability of state agencies to meet SB spending goals and extending the mandate's reach into infrastructure and services contracts — the exact categories where Prime Pathwy's property turnover and consulting services operate.

Strategic Implication: SB Certification via DGS OSDS (application in progress) is the master key. Once certified, AA Capital INC is immediately eligible to compete for a pipeline valued in the hundreds of millions annually across state agencies in Northern California. The 25% mandate is not optional for state agencies — it is enforced. This creates structural demand for certified SBs regardless of economic conditions.

Solano County Market Conditions

Solano County, anchored by Vallejo, Fairfield, Vacaville, and Benicia, represents a high-demand market for property turnover services. Key market drivers:

  • Vacancy rates: Solano County rental vacancy has remained above state average, driven by post-pandemic tenant displacement and ongoing housing cost pressures — creating continuous turnover demand for landlords and property managers
  • Eviction volume: Post-moratorium eviction filings across Solano County continue at elevated rates, creating urgent demand for professional eviction clean-out and haul-out services
  • SFH investor activity: Investor acquisition of single-family rental stock in Vallejo/Benicia corridors accelerated 2022–2025, creating a recurring client base of institutional landlords requiring documented, repeatable turnover systems
  • Vendor gap: Solano County lacks institutional-grade, documented turnover operators. The market is dominated by informal cleaning crews and uncoordinated handymen — a gap Prime Pathwy is designed to fill

Consulting Market — The $5,000 Gap

The target market for the Elite 10 Sovereign System is the Solano/Bay Area operator who is running a hauling, logistics, or painting business manually — owner-dependent, paper-based, and losing $40,000–$100,000/year in unbilled revenue and wasted admin time. This operator exists in every zip code in Northern California. The NEPQ diagnostic reveals the gap. The $5,000 system closes it.
