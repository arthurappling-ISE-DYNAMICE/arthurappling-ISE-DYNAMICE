## I. REGULATORY CONTEXT & OPPORTUNITY WINDOW

The Bay Area Air Quality Management District (BAAQMD) is opening the **Voucher Incentive Program (VIP)** in **Summer 2026** [1]. This program provides rapid-turnaround funding specifically targeted at small fleets (20 or fewer vehicles) to scrap older, high-polluting diesel or alternative-fuel trucks and replace them with new, zero-emission vehicles (ZEVs) [2]. 

For Prime Pathwy clients operating in Solano, Contra Costa, and Napa counties—especially those within SB 535-designated Disadvantaged Communities (DAC) like Vallejo—this represents a high-priority funding lane with enhanced subsidy rates.

---

## II. THE REPLACEMENT ARITHMETIC

The core of the VIP program is the mandatory scrapping of an operational, compliant baseline vehicle. Replacing older Class 4–5 trucks with CARB-approved ZEVs unlocks massive capital subsidies.

| Vehicle Class | GVWR Range | Eligible Replacement | Maximum VIP Voucher | Target Models |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Class 4–5** | 14,001–19,500 lbs | Zero-Emission Vehicle (ZEV) | **$130,000** | Isuzu NRR EV, Freightliner eM2, Motiv EPIC |
| **Class 6–8** | 19,501 lbs and above | Zero-Emission Vehicle (ZEV) | **Up to $410,000** | Freightliner eCascadia, Peterbilt 579EV |

## III. STEP-BY-STEP ACQUISITION WORKFLOW (UPS-STANDARD)

To guarantee compliance and eliminate the risk of application disqualification, operators must follow this strict, chronological workflow:

```
[1. COMPLIANCE AUDIT] ──► [2. DEALER MATCH] ──► [3. VEHICLE SELECTION] ──► [4. CONTRACT SIGNING] ──► [5. DESTRUCTION]
```

### Step 1: Baseline Compliance & Operational Audit
* **Requirement:** The vehicle to be replaced must be currently operational, registered in California, and have been driven at least 8,000 miles (or operated 250 hours) annually in the Bay Area over the past 24 months [2].
* **Compliance Check:** The fleet must be fully compliant with all CARB regulations. Direct compliance questions to CARB staff at 1-866-6DIESEL.
* **Prohibition:** **Do not place a deposit or order the replacement vehicle yet.** Any binding financial commitment made before the BAAQMD grant contract is fully executed will result in immediate disqualification [1].

### Step 2: Approved VIP Dealership Match
* **Requirement:** VIP applications cannot be submitted directly by the fleet owner. They must be submitted through a BAAQMD-approved VIP Truck Dealership [3].
* **Action:** Partner with an approved dealer (refer to the BAAQMD dealership registry or participate in BAAQMD training).

### Step 3: Vehicle Selection & Telemetry Setup
* **Requirement:** Select a CARB-approved ZEV box truck (Class 4–5).
* **Modernization Integration:** Ensure the selected vehicle is pre-configured with local GPS and digital manifest telemetry to support UPS-standard **Package Flow Technology (PFT)** and **DIAD-standard** mobile driver tracking upon delivery.

### Step 4: Grant Agreement Execution
* **Action:** The dealer submits the application package to BAAQMD. Once approved, the fleet owner, dealer, and BAAQMD execute the formal three-party funding agreement.

### Step 5: Destruction of Baseline Vehicle
* **Requirement:** The old diesel vehicle must be delivered to a BAAQMD-approved dismantler to be scrapped and destroyed (engine block must be bored, and frame rails cut) within 60 days of taking delivery of the new ZEV.
