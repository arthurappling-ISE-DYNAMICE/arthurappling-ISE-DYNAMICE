## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This playbook governs all outbound engagement targeting the 12 identified independent junk removal and hauling operators in Solano County, California. These operators — including but not limited to Ready Now Junk Removal and 3 Kings Hauling — share a common operational profile: they generate inbound demand primarily through Yelp, manage all booking and scheduling through manual phone calls, and lack any automated intake infrastructure. The consequence is a chronic, invisible revenue leak that compounds daily.

The engagement strategy is built entirely on the **NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questions)** framework developed by Jeremy Miner. NEPQ does not sell through pressure or manipulation. It sells by guiding the prospect to **self-diagnose their own pain**, articulate the consequences of inaction in their own words, and arrive at the buying decision internally. The salesperson's role is to ask precisely calibrated questions, listen with surgical intent, and allow the prospect's own emotional reality to close the sale.

The target outcome is a booked discovery call that transitions into a $5,000 Sovereign Intake System Installation — a one-time infrastructure build that automates lead capture, instant quoting, scheduling, and follow-up for the operator's entire business.

## SECTION 2 — THE 2-MINUTE COLD CALL SCRIPT (NEPQ CONNECTION PHASE)

### 2.1 Strategic Intent

The cold call is not a pitch. It is a **diagnostic conversation opener**. The NEPQ Connection Phase is designed to accomplish three things in under 120 seconds:

1. **Disarm** the prospect's default resistance to salespeople by sounding like a peer, not a vendor.
2. **Establish situational credibility** by demonstrating specific knowledge of their operational context.
3. **Plant the emotional anchor** — a Connection Question that causes the owner to re-live the financial anxiety of a specific, recent pain event (a cancellation, a missed booking, or an unsigned contract dispute).

The representative must never pitch in the first call. The goal is a 10-minute audit appointment.

---

### 2.2 Pre-Call Intelligence Checklist

Before dialing, confirm the following from Yelp and any available public data:

- [ ] Business name and owner's first name confirmed
- [ ] Number of Yelp reviews and average response time noted
- [ ] Any visible negative reviews mentioning "didn't call back" or "went with someone else"
- [ ] Service area (Fairfield, Vallejo, Vacaville, Benicia, Dixon, etc.) confirmed
- [ ] Approximate truck count or team size estimated from photos

---

### 2.3 The Full Cold Call Script

---

**[0:00 – 0:15] THE DISARMING OPENER**

*Tone: Calm, slightly uncertain, peer-level. Never sound like a telemarketer. Speak as if you are calling a colleague to ask a quick question.*

> "Hey, is this [First Name]? Hey — it's Arthur with Prime Pathwy. I was actually just going through some of the hauling volume data coming out of Solano County right now... and honestly, I wasn't sure if I should even be reaching out to you, or if you guys are already fully booked out for the month?"

*[Wait for response. They will almost always say they can take more work, or that it depends.]*

---

**[0:15 – 0:45] THE SITUATION QUESTION**

*Tone: Genuinely curious. You are diagnosing, not interrogating.*

> "Got it. The reason I ask is we've been working with a handful of service operators in the area who are trying to grow, but they keep running into the same bottleneck — they're getting solid Yelp traffic, but the manual call-back process is creating a gap between the inquiry and the actual booked job. Just out of curiosity, how are you currently handling it when two or three quote requests come in at the same time while your truck is already out on a job?"

*[Let them explain. Do not interrupt. Their answer will reveal the exact pain point.]*

---

**[0:45 – 1:30] THE CONNECTION QUESTION — THE EMOTIONAL ANCHOR**

*This is the most critical moment in the call. The Connection Question is designed to make the owner re-live a specific, recent instance of financial loss. Speak slowly and with genuine empathy. You are not attacking — you are acknowledging a shared reality.*

> "I hear you. So when you're out on a job in Fairfield or Vacaville, and you get a Yelp message or a call you can't get to right away... has there been a situation recently — maybe in the last few weeks — where a customer ended up going with another hauler just because they got back to them faster? Or maybe you had a quote dispute because nothing was locked in with a formal system, and the job just... fell apart?"

*[Pause. Let them answer. They will almost always recall a specific incident. This is the emotional anchor.]*

> "Man. How did that feel when that happened? And what did that cost you that day — just roughly?"

*[Let them put a number on it. Their own number is more powerful than any statistic you could provide.]*

---

**[1:30 – 2:00] THE SOFT PIVOT TO NEXT STEPS**

*Tone: Low-pressure, collaborative. You are not closing — you are opening a door.*

> "Look, [First Name] — it sounds like you're doing solid work out there, and the demand is clearly there. But it sounds like the intake process might be creating a ceiling on how much of that demand you're actually capturing. I'm honestly not sure yet if what we build would even be the right fit for your operation. But would you be open to a quick 10-minute audit next Tuesday or Wednesday — just so I can look at the numbers and tell you honestly whether there's a gap worth fixing?"

*[If yes: book the appointment and confirm with a calendar invite. If hesitant: proceed to the objection handling section below.]*

---

### 2.4 Objection Handling (NEPQ-Style)

NEPQ objection handling never argues. It responds to every objection with a question that invites the prospect to examine their own position.

| Common Objection | NEPQ Response |
|---|---|
| *"I'm not interested."* | "That's completely fair. Can I ask — is it that you're not interested in solving the missed booking problem, or just not sure if now is the right time?" |
| *"I already have a system."* | "I appreciate that. What does your system do when a lead comes in at 7pm on a Saturday while you're finishing a job?" |
| *"I can't afford it right now."* | "I understand. What's it costing you every month to keep running it the way you're running it now?" |
| *"I don't have time for this."* | "That makes sense. Is the reason you don't have time because you're spending it on things the system would handle for you?" |
| *"Send me an email."* | "Absolutely. Before I do — what would need to be in that email for it to actually be worth your time to open it?" |

## SECTION 4 — STRATEGIC PRICING TRANSITION FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 The Psychology of the $5,000 Investment

The single greatest obstacle to closing the Sovereign Intake System at $5,000 is not price resistance — it is **frame resistance**. The prospect is comparing $5,000 to their current monthly revenue and experiencing sticker shock. The representative's job is to **reframe the comparison entirely**.

The prospect must stop comparing $5,000 to their monthly revenue and start comparing it to the cost of *not* having the system. This is accomplished through a four-step pricing transition framework executed during the discovery call.

---

### 4.2 The Four-Step Pricing Transition

**Step 1: Quantify the Bleed (The Diagnostic)**

Before price is ever mentioned, the representative must establish the prospect's personal revenue leak number using their own data from the conversation.

> "Based on what you shared with me — you're missing roughly 3 to 4 bookings a week because of the manual process. At your average ticket of [their number], that's approximately [calculated monthly loss] a month in revenue your business is generating the demand for but not capturing. Does that math feel accurate to you?"

*[Let them confirm the number. Their confirmation is ownership.]*

---

**Step 2: The Consequence Question (The Urgency Anchor)**

> "If you don't address this, and you keep running the intake manually for the next 12 months — what does that cost you? Not just in dollars, but in the time you're spending doing tasks that a system should be handling?"

*[Let them answer. They will articulate the cost in both financial and personal terms.]*

---

**Step 3: The Sovereign System Reveal (The Reframe)**

> "What we install is called the Sovereign Intake System. It is a one-time infrastructure build — not a subscription, not a monthly fee. It handles automated lead capture, instant quoting, calendar scheduling, and follow-up sequences. Once it is installed, it runs autonomously. The investment is $5,000, paid once."

*[Pause. Let the number land.]*

> "Based on the revenue gap we just calculated — at [their monthly loss] a month — the system pays for itself in approximately [calculated payback period in weeks]. After that, every job it captures is pure recovered revenue."

---

**Step 4: The Time-Freedom Anchor (The Close)**

> "Here is the real question, [First Name]: You told me earlier that you're spending [X hours] a week on calls, scheduling, and follow-up. What is your personal time worth to you? Because what this system is really doing — beyond the revenue recovery — is buying back those hours permanently. Does it make more sense to continue paying [monthly loss] every month in lost revenue and lost personal time, or to make a one-time investment of $5,000 and own your intake process completely?"

*[This is a genuine question, not a rhetorical device. Wait for their answer.]*

## APPENDIX B — CORE NEPQ PRINCIPLES FOR REPRESENTATIVE CALIBRATION

The following principles govern every interaction in this playbook. Representatives must internalize these before executing any outbound contact.

**Questions gather, analyze, and diagnose.** Every question asked in the NEPQ framework serves a diagnostic function. The representative is not filling time — they are building a clinical picture of the prospect's pain.

**Statements create doubt; questions create belief.** If the representative asserts that the prospect is losing revenue, the prospect may resist. If the representative asks a question that causes the prospect to calculate and state the loss themselves, the prospect believes it completely — because it came from their own mouth.

**Consequence questions build urgency without pressure.** The prospect must feel the weight of inaction as a natural conclusion of their own reasoning, not as a fear tactic deployed by a salesperson. The question "What are you going to do if nothing changes?" is not a threat — it is an invitation to think clearly.

**The representative's emotional state is the prospect's emotional state.** Calm confidence is non-negotiable. If the representative sounds uncertain, rushed, or eager to close, the prospect's defense mechanism activates immediately. Speak slowly. Pause after questions. Let silence work.

**Never pitch before the pain is fully surfaced.** Introducing the Sovereign Intake System before the prospect has articulated their own pain in their own words is the single most common failure mode in this engagement. The system is never revealed until the prospect has confirmed the revenue gap and expressed a desire to change.
