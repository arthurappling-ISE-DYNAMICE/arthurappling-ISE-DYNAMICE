## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended ("IRC"), Section 62(a)(2)(A), and Treasury Regulation §1.62-2, permit a corporation to reimburse employees and officers for ordinary and necessary business expenses on a tax-free basis, provided such reimbursements are made pursuant to an "accountable plan" meeting the requirements set forth therein;

**WHEREAS**, an accountable plan requires: (1) a business connection — the expense must have a legitimate business purpose directly related to the Corporation's trade or business; (2) substantiation — employees must provide adequate records within a reasonable time period establishing the amount, date, place, and business purpose of each expense; and (3) return of excess advances — any reimbursement or advance in excess of documented substantiated expenses must be returned to the Corporation within a reasonable time period;

**WHEREAS**, reimbursements made under a qualifying accountable plan are excluded from the employee's gross income, are not subject to FICA or income tax withholding, and are fully deductible by the Corporation as ordinary and necessary business expenses under IRC §162;

**WHEREAS**, the Board of Directors has determined that it is in the best interest of the Corporation and its shareholders to adopt a formal Accountable Plan to govern all employee business expense reimbursements;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** as set forth below.

---

## RESOLUTION 1: ADOPTION OF ACCOUNTABLE PLAN

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation hereby formally adopts the following **Accountable Plan for Employee Business Expense Reimbursements** (the "Plan"), effective **May 20, 2026**, which shall govern all reimbursements of business expenses incurred by employees, officers, shareholders, and directors of the Corporation:

---

## ACCOUNTABLE PLAN FOR EMPLOYEE BUSINESS EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENTS

### Article I — Purpose and Scope

**1.1 Purpose.** The purpose of this Plan is to establish a formal accountable plan within the meaning of IRC §62(a)(2)(A) and Treas. Reg. §1.62-2, under which the Corporation will reimburse employees, officers, and shareholder-employees for ordinary and necessary business expenses incurred on behalf of the Corporation.

**1.2 Scope.** This Plan applies to all employees, officers, directors, and shareholder-employees of AA Capital INC (collectively, "Covered Persons") who incur reimbursable business expenses in the course of performing services for the Corporation.

**1.3 Legal Authority.**
- **IRC §62(a)(2)(A)** — Exclusion for reimbursed employee business expenses under an accountable plan
- **IRC §162(a)** — Deductibility of ordinary and necessary business expenses
- **Treas. Reg. §1.62-2** — Requirements for accountable plans
- **IRS Publication 463** — Travel, Gift, and Car Expenses

---

### Article II — Eligible Expenses

**2.1 Reimbursable Expense Categories.** The following categories of expenses are eligible for reimbursement under this Plan, provided they satisfy the business purpose and substantiation requirements of Article III:

**(a) Home Office Expenses**
Expenses attributable to the portion of the Covered Person's personal residence used regularly and exclusively as the principal place of business for the Corporation, including:
- Prorated rent or mortgage interest (based on square footage percentage)
- Prorated utilities (electricity, internet, gas)
- Repairs and maintenance attributable to the home office area
- Depreciation of home office space (if applicable)

**(b) Technology and Communications**
- Business-use portion of personal cell phone and mobile data plan
- Business-use portion of home internet service
- Software subscriptions and SaaS tools used for Corporation business
- Computer hardware and peripherals used in Corporation operations
- Cloud storage and technology infrastructure services

**(c) Business Travel**
- Airfare, train, and ground transportation for business travel
- Lodging at reasonable rates during business travel
- Meals during overnight business travel (subject to 50% limitation under IRC §274(n))
- Mileage for business use of personal vehicle at the IRS standard mileage rate in effect for the calendar year
- Parking and tolls incurred during business travel

**(d) Business Meals (Client / Business Associate Entertainment)**
- Meals with clients, prospective clients, vendors, or business associates at which substantive business is discussed, subject to the 50% limitation under IRC §274(n)
- Business purpose, names of attendees, and business relationship must be documented

**(e) Professional Development and Education**
- Continuing education courses, seminars, workshops, and conferences directly related to maintaining or improving skills required for the Covered Person's role with the Corporation
- Professional books, publications, and subscriptions
- Professional license renewal fees and dues to professional associations

**(f) Office Supplies and Materials**
- Supplies consumed in the performance of Corporation business
- Postage, shipping, and courier services for business purposes

**(g) Home Rental — IRC §280A(g) (The Augusta Rule)**
The Corporation may rent the personal residence of an employee or officer for bona fide business meetings, board sessions, executive strategy retreats, or corporate training purposes for up to **fourteen (14) days per calendar year** pursuant to IRC §280A(g). The rental rate must be at fair market value, supported by contemporaneous comparable rental quotes from third-party commercial venues. The renting employee or officer shall not recognize income from such payments, provided the 14-day limit is strictly observed.

**2.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses.** The following expenses are expressly excluded from this Plan:
- Personal, living, or family expenses as defined under IRC §262
- Expenses for commuting between the Covered Person's residence and the Corporation's principal office
- Entertainment expenses that are not deductible under IRC §274 (post-TCJA 2017)
- Expenses lacking a legitimate business purpose
- Capital expenditures that must be capitalized under IRC §263
- Fines, penalties, or illegal payments

---

### Article III — Substantiation Requirements

**3.1 Required Documentation.** All reimbursement requests must be substantiated by adequate records as required by IRC §274(d) and Treas. Reg. §1.274-5. For each expense, the Covered Person must provide:

- **Amount** of the expense
- **Date** the expense was incurred
- **Place** (location, vendor, or destination)
- **Business purpose** — a written description of the specific business reason for the expense
- **Attendees** — for meals, the names and business relationship of all persons present

**3.2 Receipt Requirement.** Receipts, invoices, or other documentary evidence must be provided for:
- Any single expense of **$75 or more**
- All lodging expenses, regardless of amount
- All travel expenses

**3.3 Mileage Log Requirement.** For business use of a personal vehicle, the Covered Person must maintain a contemporaneous mileage log documenting:
- Date of each trip
- Origin and destination
- Business purpose of the trip
- Miles driven

**3.4 Expense Report Submission Deadline.** All expense reports must be submitted to the Corporation within **60 days** after the expense is incurred. Expense reports submitted after 60 days may be treated as taxable compensation to the Covered Person at the Corporation's discretion.

---

### Article IV — Reimbursement Procedures

**4.1 Expense Report Form.** All reimbursement requests must be submitted on the Corporation's designated expense report form (or an equivalent written substantiation document) signed by the Covered Person under penalty of perjury confirming the accuracy and business purpose of all claimed expenses.

**4.2 Approval.** All expense reports must be reviewed and approved by an authorized officer of the Corporation prior to reimbursement. For the sole officer/shareholder, self-approved reports must be retained in the corporate records.

**4.3 Reimbursement Method.** Approved reimbursements shall be paid via:
- Direct deposit to the Covered Person's designated bank account, or
- Corporate check

Reimbursements shall **not** be included on the Covered Person's Form W-2 as taxable wages, provided the Plan's substantiation requirements are fully met.

**4.4 Corporate Deductibility.** Reimbursements made under this Plan shall be recorded as ordinary and necessary business expense deductions of the Corporation under IRC §162, categorized by expense type on the Corporation's books and records.

---

### Article V — Return of Excess Advances

**5.1 Excess Advances.** If the Corporation provides any advance or allowance to a Covered Person in anticipation of a business expense, any advance in excess of the documented, substantiated expense must be returned to the Corporation within **120 days** after the expense is paid or incurred.

**5.2 Failure to Return.** Any advance not returned within the 120-day period will be treated as taxable wages to the Covered Person and will be included on the Covered Person's Form W-2 for the applicable calendar year.

---

### Article VI — Recordkeeping

**6.1 Corporate Records.** The Corporation shall retain all approved expense reports, receipts, and supporting documentation for a minimum of **7 years** from the date of filing of the tax return to which the expenses relate, consistent with the IRS statute of limitations under IRC §6501.

**6.2 Digital Records.** Digital receipts, scanned documents, and electronic expense reports are acceptable provided they are legible, complete, and stored in a manner that ensures their availability upon request during an IRS examination.

**6.3 Audit Preparedness.** The Corporation's bookkeeper or accountant shall maintain monthly categorization of all expense reimbursements in the Corporation's accounting system, with line-item coding by expense type for financial statement and tax return preparation purposes.

---

### Article VII — Amendment and Termination

**7.1 Amendment.** This Plan may be amended by resolution of the Board of Directors at any time, provided that any amendment shall not adversely affect the tax treatment of properly substantiated expenses reimbursed prior to the effective date of the amendment.

**7.2 Termination.** This Plan may be terminated by resolution of the Board of Directors at any time. Termination shall not affect the treatment of reimbursements properly made prior to the termination date.

---

### Article VIII — State Compliance Matrix (California FTB Conformity)

**8.1 Mandatory Franchise Tax Conformity.** Pursuant to California Revenue and Taxation Code (R&TC), the Corporation acknowledges that while it operates as a pass-through entity for federal income tax purposes under Subchapter S, the State of California imposes a mandatory **1.5% Franchise Tax** on the net income of the Corporation, subject to a statutory **$800 annual minimum tax** requirement under **R&TC §23153**.

**8.2 Local Nexus and Principal Base.** The Corporation formally binds this Accountable Plan to its established local nexus and principal executive office located at **425 Virginia St STE B, Vallejo, CA 94590**, operating under official California Department of General Services Small Business Certification ID: **2052045**.

**8.3 Quarterly Compliance and Reserve Discipline.** To prevent year-end cash-flow distortion and ensure audit-readiness under California Franchise Tax Board (FTB) scrutiny, the Corporation shall calculate and reserve its estimated state tax liabilities on a **quarterly basis**. Estimated tax payments shall be made in accordance with the FTB's prescribed schedule to avoid underpayment penalties under **R&TC §19136**.

**8.4 Decoupling Exclusions.** The Corporation's bookkeeping infrastructure shall maintain separate schedules where California tax law decouples from federal provisions — including accelerated depreciation under **IRC §168(k)** — ensuring that reimbursements made under this Plan do not cross into non-deductible state-level adjustments. All expense categorizations shall be reviewed for California conformity at the time of each quarterly close.
