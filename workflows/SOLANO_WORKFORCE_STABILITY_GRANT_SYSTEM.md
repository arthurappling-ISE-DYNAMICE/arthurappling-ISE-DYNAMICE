# PRIME PATHWY: SOLANO WORKFORCE STABILITY GRANT SYSTEM
## LAYOFF AVERSION & OPERATIONAL PIVOT PROPOSAL (CATEGORY A)

> **Sovereign System Statement:** This system engineers a bulletproof, audit-ready grant application for the Solano Workforce Stability Grant, securing up to $25,000 in non-dilutive federal Rapid Response funding to install UPS-standard operational systems and prevent regional layoffs.

---

## I. FUNDING OPPORTUNITY & ELIGIBILITY

The Workforce Development Board of Solano County (WDB), in partnership with the Solano-Napa Small Business Development Center (SBDC), administers the **Solano Workforce Stability Grant Program** [1]. Funded by the federal **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)** Dislocated Worker Program, this initiative supports business stabilization and layoff aversion [2].

For local logistics, hauling, and industrial painting operators, this grant provides a critical mechanism to fund technology upgrades, internal process improvements, and worker upskilling.

---

The program is structured into distinct funding opportunities based on the specific regional closures impacting the business. To secure maximum funding, applications must align with the correct economic shock corridor:

## III. THE UPS-GRADE LAYOFF AVERSION PLAN (THE SYSTEM DESIGN)

A successful application requires a highly detailed, written **Layoff Aversion Plan** demonstrating how the requested funds will prevent workforce reductions [1]. 

Rather than proposing generic "marketing" or "general operating expenses" (which are strictly non-allowable), Prime Pathwy structures the plan around installing a **UPS-standard digital dispatch and routing manifest system** [3]:

### 1. The Technology Pivot (Allowable Use)
* **Action:** Purchase of ruggedized mobile driver interfaces (DIAD-standard) and a centralized dispatch software license (PFT-standard).
* **Justification:** Automating the scheduling and manifest flow directly increases route efficiency, allowing the business to capture new contract opportunities and absorb the economic shock of regional closures.

### 2. Employee Upskilling (Allowable Use)
* **Action:** Comprehensive training for dispatchers and drivers on algorithmic route sequencing (ORION-Lite) and digital proof-of-delivery capture.
* **Justification:** Training existing employees to operate advanced logistics technology directly prevents layoffs by transitioning them from low-value manual administrators to high-efficiency systems operators.

## IV. STEP-BY-STEP APPLICATION WORKFLOW

```
[1. ELIGIBILITY AUDIT] ──► [2. CORE DOCUMENTS] ──► [3. BUDGET FORMULATION] ──► [4. PORTAL SUBMISSION] ──► [5. POST-AWARD REPORT]
```

### Step 1: Eligibility Verification
* Must have a physical business location in Solano County [1].
* Must hold a valid business license in the city of operation (e.g., Vallejo, Benicia, Fairfield).
* Must have one or more employees (excluding the owner). Sole proprietors without employees are **not eligible** [1].
* Must commit to retaining at least 50% of the workforce for a minimum of six (6) months following receipt of funds [1].

### Step 2: Core Document Preparation
* [ ] Copy of a valid local Business License.
* [ ] Completed IRS Form W-9 (signed and dated) [2].
* [ ] Documented proof of economic impact (revenue decline, loss of customers, or vendor disruption resulting from the refinery, dry dock, or brewery closures).

### Step 3: Budget Formulation (Matte Black & Gold Compliant)
The budget must strictly exclude non-allowable uses (such as wages, rent, utilities, or vehicle purchases) [1]:

| Budget Line Item | Category | Cost | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sovereign Dispatch Software** | Technology Upgrade | $4,500 | Digital manifest system to automate scheduling and replace lost revenue. |
| **Ruggedized Driver Tablets** | Computer Equipment | $3,000 | DIAD-standard mobile driver interfaces for digital signatures and GPS tracking. |
| **Algorithmic Routing Training** | Employee Training | $2,500 | Upskilling drivers on ORION-Lite route sequencing to reduce fuel waste. |
| **Total Grant Request** | **Layoff Aversion** | **$10,000** | **Mare Island Dry Dock Corridor / Business Stabilization** |

### Step 4: Online Portal Submission
Submit the complete application via the Solano WDB online portal [2]. Monitor email closely—awardees have only **two (2) business days** to confirm acceptance upon notification [1].

### Step 5: Post-Award 6-Month Reporting Compliance
Awarded businesses must submit a detailed report 6 months post-disbursement, including:
* Narrative summary of completed activities.
* Full receipt documentation (invoices, proof of payment) [1].
* Verified employee headcount showing at least 50% retention [1].
