# Master Pathwy SOP — Risks & Limitations
**Owner:** Arthur F. Appling Sr. — AA Capital Inc. / Prime Pathwy

---

## Known Limitations

- **12–18 month engagement is a significant time commitment** — each engagement requires sustained attention across 4 phases. At 10 active clients, this is the primary capacity constraint.
- **`/Scaffold` and `/Prime` skills required** — Steps 4 and 2 respectively depend on these skills. Manual execution is possible but slower and less consistent.
- **Deliverable quality is manual** — all 15 deliverables require human judgment and domain knowledge. No deliverable is automated end-to-end.
- **Matte Black + Gold aesthetic standard** — all client-facing documents must meet the `#0B0B0B`/`#C9A646` brand standard. This is a visual QC requirement on every deliverable.
- **WAT framework compliance required** — all deliverables stored in `/workflows`, `/agents`, `/tools` per the client's managed GitHub repository structure. Misplaced files violate the framework.
- **Step 15 requires 3-hour handoff session** — cannot be done asynchronously. Requires scheduling with client.

---

## Failure Modes

| Scenario | What Breaks | Mitigation |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Deliverable not in client GitHub repo | WAT compliance failure; not Institutional Grade | Never mark step complete without verifying file placement |
| Client skips Steps 1–2 ("just start building") | Chaos Cost Statement missing; pricing justification gone | Steps 1–2 are non-negotiable — diagnose before building, always |
| Engagement runs past 18 months | Scope creep; engagement economics deteriorate | Set 18-month hard boundary at start; Handoff is the terminal state |
| Client refuses Step 15 transfer ("just keep managing it") | Engagement dependency created; violates framework | Step 15 is mandatory — 100% client ownership is the mission fulfilled |
| Tool stack not documented (Step 7) | Client cannot operate tools after handoff | Tool Stack Registry must be complete before Step 8 begins |

## Deprecation Risk

**Low.** The 15-step sequence is based on operational principles that are industry and platform agnostic. Steps may be adapted for new industries beyond hauling/painting/logistics, but the sequence structure is stable.
