# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Property managers, landlords, and housing authorities in Solano County (Vallejo, CA and surrounding cities). They are mid-session — comparing vendors after an eviction or vacancy notice, under time pressure, needing a provider who is fast, documented, and commercially credible. They do not want to be sold; they want to be convinced.

## Product Purpose

Prime Pathwy delivers professional property turnovers: cleaned, hauled, painted, and documented in 3–5 business days. The site exists to convert a skeptical landlord or property manager into a same-day quote request. Success is a phone call or form submission — not a brand impression.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign. Precise. Unafraid.

Tone: institutional authority, not warmth. The brand speaks the way a well-capitalized contractor speaks — confident, specific, never desperate. Every word earns its place.

## Anti-references

- **Generic cleaning company**: Cheap clip-art, light-blue/green palette, bubble fonts, low-authority aesthetic.
- **Loud contractor site**: Neon CTAs, stock photos of smiling workers, cluttered layout, noise-first hierarchy.
- **Crypto/NFT dark UI**: Neon on black, excessive glow effects, gradient text everywhere — dark but cheap.
- **Corporate SaaS**: White + blue enterprise aesthetic (Salesforce, ServiceNow energy) — zero soul, zero authority.

## Design Principles

1. **Authority over warmth.** Every decision projects institutional confidence. Never sacrifice commanding presence for approachability.
2. **Concrete over hype.** Real numbers, real scope, real timelines. No vague promises, no filler copy, no aspirational language without evidence.
3. **Silence as power.** Negative space and restraint communicate premium. Noise belongs to low-trust brands.
4. **Documented equals trusted.** Before/after, evidence, paper trails — the product IS the documentation. The design surfaces proof, not claims.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. High-contrast gold-on-dark satisfies contrast ratios for primary text. Reduced motion preference respected for any scroll or entry animations.
