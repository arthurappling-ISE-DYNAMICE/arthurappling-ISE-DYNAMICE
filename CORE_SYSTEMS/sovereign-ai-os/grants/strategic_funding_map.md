## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Prime Pathwy's service lines — janitorial, waste hauling, debris removal, tire cleanup, facility maintenance, disaster cleanup, commercial property turnover, and environmental services — align with multiple active and recurring government funding programs. The primary strategic insight is that **Prime Pathwy cannot directly apply for most of these grants**, but can generate significant revenue as a **subcontractor or service provider** to entities that do receive grant funding.

This map identifies the three primary revenue pathways and the specific programs that support each.

---

## PATHWAY 1 — DISASTER RESPONSE CONTRACTOR (HIGHEST URGENCY)

**Revenue Model:** Government entities receive FEMA Public Assistance funds for disaster cleanup. They hire local contractors. Prime Pathwy performs the work and gets paid by the government entity.

**Why This Matters:** The LA Wildfire disaster declaration (DR-4856-CA, January 2025) demonstrates that California is in an active disaster cycle. NorCal is subject to wildfires, flooding, and storms. When a declaration is issued, local vendors are prioritized.

| Program | Funding Source | Your Role | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| FEMA Public Assistance - Category A (Debris Removal) | FEMA | Subcontractor to government entity | Register in SAM.gov Disaster Response Registry |
| FEMA Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP) | FEMA | Subcontractor to government entity | Register with Army Corps of Engineers Contractor Registry |
| FEMA Advance Contracts | FEMA | Prime or sub | Submit FEMA Vendor Profile Form |
| CalOES Emergency Contracts | State of California | Prime or sub | Register with CalOES; build county emergency manager relationships |

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Register in SAM.gov at https://sam.gov — include Disaster Response Registry opt-in
2. Submit FEMA Vendor Profile Form at https://www.fema.gov/business-industry/doing-business
3. Register with Army Corps of Engineers Contractor Registry
4. Contact Solano, Sacramento, Contra Costa, Alameda, and San Joaquin county emergency management offices
5. Maintain 24/7 emergency response capability documentation
