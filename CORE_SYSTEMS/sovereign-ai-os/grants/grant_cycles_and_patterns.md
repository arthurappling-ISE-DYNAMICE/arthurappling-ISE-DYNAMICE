## PATTERN ANALYSIS

### CalRecycle Grant Patterns

CalRecycle operates on a **fiscal year cycle (July 1 – June 30)**. Most grant programs open applications in **August–October** for the current fiscal year and close in **October–February**. Awards are typically announced in **December–April**. Grant terms run through **June 30 of the following fiscal year** (typically 2-3 years).

Key pattern: CalRecycle programs are **highly competitive** relative to available funding. The Illegal Disposal Site Abatement program typically receives $2M–$5M in requests against $1M in available funds. **Early application and strong project documentation are critical.**

### FEMA Disaster Patterns

FEMA contracting is **event-driven** and cannot be predicted by calendar. However, California's disaster history shows:
- Average of 3-5 major disaster declarations per year in California
- Wildfire season: June–November (highest risk)
- Flood/storm season: November–March
- The 2025 LA Wildfire (DR-4856-CA) generated hundreds of millions in cleanup contracts

**Key insight:** Local vendors in the affected area are strongly preferred. Prime Pathwy's NorCal presence is a strategic asset for NorCal disaster events.

### EPA Brownfields Patterns

EPA Brownfields grants follow a **federal fiscal year cycle (October 1 – September 30)**. Applications typically open in **November** and close in **January–February**. Awards are announced in **April–June**. The FY 2026 cycle closed January 28, 2026. FY 2027 is expected to open November 2026.

## HISTORICAL AWARD ANALYSIS

### CalRecycle Illegal Disposal Site Abatement

| Cycle | Fiscal Year | Available Funding | Typical Requests | Competition Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 104 | 2025-26 | $1,000,000 | $2M–$5M | Very High |
| 106 | 2025-26 | Remaining from Cycle 104 | Variable | Very High |
| 2026-27 | 2026-27 | ~$1,000,000 (estimated) | $2M–$5M | Very High |

**Award Pattern:** Grants are awarded to public entities (counties, cities) that have identified specific illegal dumping sites. Awards range from $50,000 to $500,000 per site. Counties with active enforcement programs and documented sites have higher success rates.
